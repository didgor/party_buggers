## Why HouseWise?

Finding student accommodation should be exciting, not a gamble.

As students, finding accommodation can feel like one of the most stressful parts of university. You're expected to sign a legally binding contract, understand complicated tenancy agreements, trust that a landlord is legitimate, and commit to paying thousands of pounds, often within just a few days. Most students simply don't have the experience to know whether they're making the right decision.

Every year, thousands of students sign tenancy agreements without fully understanding what they're agreeing to. They often don't know whether the rent is actually competitive, whether hidden costs will make the property more expensive, whether the landlord has a poor reputation, or even whether they're renting from someone who is properly certified. Important information exists, but it's scattered across different platforms, making it difficult for students to make informed decisions.



HouseWise brings all of this information into one place.



HouseWise is a Student Housing Transparency Assistant that supports students before, during, and after their tenancy, helping them make informed decisions and understand their rights throughout the entire renting journey. Before signing, students can upload a property listing or tenancy agreement. The assistant explains contracts in plain English, highlights hidden clauses, vague wording, unusual terms, unfair fees, and potential risks, while flagging anything that should be questioned before signing. It also explains concepts such as joint and several liability, helping students understand how a joint tenancy could leave them responsible if another housemate stops paying rent.



The assistant also identifies potential pressure tactics used by landlords or letting agents, encouraging students to take time to understand what they're signing rather than rushing into a legally binding agreement. HouseWise compares similar nearby properties, estimates the true monthly cost of living by including bills, council tax (where applicable), and other hidden expenses, helping students understand whether they're genuinely getting good value rather than simply choosing the cheapest advertised rent.



The platform also brings together information currently scattered across Facebook groups, Reddit discussions, Google Reviews, university accommodation forums, and other public sources, allowing students to see previous tenants' experiences with the property, landlord, or letting company before committing. To improve landlord transparency, HouseWise verifies publicly available landlord and property information where possible, checking whether the landlord or letting company appears to meet relevant legal requirements, holds the appropriate registrations or licences, and whether properties requiring HMO licences appear to be correctly licensed.



Support doesn't stop once the tenancy begins.



During a tenancy, students can log maintenance issues such as mould, damp, broken heating, leaks, or structural problems, creating a timeline of evidence with photo uploads and communication records. If repairs are ignored or legal obligations aren't being met, the assistant explains the student's rights, drafts emails, recommends who to contact next, and guides them through the appropriate escalation process.



As students approach the end of their tenancy, HouseWise also reviews proposed deposit deductions, highlighting potentially unfair charges such as professional cleaning, repainting normal wear and tear, or replacing items that were already damaged before the tenancy. If necessary, it can help students prepare evidence to dispute these deductions.



The assistant also helps students understand situations such as illegal eviction, explaining when landlords cannot ask tenants to leave, change locks, or remove belongings without following the correct legal process, while signposting students to the appropriate support services if these situations occur.



Our goal isn't to replace legal advice. It's to make one of the most confusing and opaque parts of student life far more transparent, so students can make informed decisions, understand their rights, and feel confident throughout their entire renting journey instead of only realising something was wrong once it's too late.
